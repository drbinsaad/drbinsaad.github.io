Safety
  • Units are NOT interchangeable between products. BOTOX, Xeomin, Dysport and Myobloc/Neurobloc each have their own unit system — never substitute dose-for-dose.
  • Sialorrhea injection is off-label (except the incobotulinumtoxinA pediatric licence) and has no consensus protocol. Figures are licence schedules or literature ranges, not validated formulae.
  • Inject under ultrasound guidance; watch for dysphagia and other adverse effects after treatment.
Type B toxin (rimabotulinumtoxinB). Myobloc / Neurobloc is a different molecule with its own unit system — type A and type B units are NOT convertible. Doses (thousands of units) are far larger than type-A products by design. Double-check the product and the unit count.

Pre-injection screening — confirm before injecting

Absolute contraindications
  • Myasthenia gravis
  • Lambert-Eaton syndrome
  • Prior hypersensitivity to any botulinum product
Relative / caution
  • Aminoglycosides (gentamicin/tobramycin/amikacin/neomycin)
  • Neuromuscular blockers (rocuronium/vecuronium/succinylcholine)
  • Polymyxins / clindamycin / magnesium sulfate
  • Pregnancy / lactation
  • Active infection at injection site
  • Significant dysphagia / aspiration risk

Reminders

  • Onset 48–72 h, peak 1–2 wk, duration 3–9 mo
  • Aspirate before each injection (facial artery near submandibular gland)
  • Ultrasound guidance preferred in children; scale depth to gland
  • Record baseline drooling score
Doses are product-specific and not interchangeable; verify against the current product label and local protocol.
