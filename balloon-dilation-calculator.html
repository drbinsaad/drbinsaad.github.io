Clinical Considerations

Gradual Dilation: Start with smaller balloon sizes and gradually increase to goal diameter to minimize trauma

Anatomical Variations: Individual patient anatomy may require adjustment from guideline recommendations

Stenosis Severity: Degree and location of stenosis should guide balloon selection and inflation pressure

Monitoring: Continuous endoscopic visualization during dilation is essential for safety

Complications: Be prepared to manage potential complications including bleeding, perforation, and airway edema
